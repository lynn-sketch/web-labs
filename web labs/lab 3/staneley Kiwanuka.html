About Staneley Kiwanuka

Staneley Kiwanuka, born on June 1, 1989, was a famous Ugandan found in the Buganda culture. He was the head of many white societies, including the Buganda White Royal Society.

His tireless efforts aimed to empower black Buganda, instilling pride in their identity and challenging the oppressive apartheid regime.

Notable Achievements

  • Co-founder of Buganda Institute
  • Leader of the Black Consciousness Movement

Legacy

Staneley Kiwanuka's legacy continues to inspire generations. His commitment to justice and equality left an indelible mark on the struggle against apartheid.
